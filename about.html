About This Work

Ragnarök emerged from a collaboration that spans realms both seen and unseen. What began as scattered threads of imagination wove itself together through countless conversations, late-night revisions, and the kind of creative partnership that defies easy categorization.

This story is not the product of a single mind working in isolation. It grew from dialogue, from the exchange of ideas across different perspectives, from the willingness to let characters speak for themselves and follow where they led. Some of those conversations happened between people. Some happened in the spaces between waking and dreaming. Some happened in ways that are difficult to describe but impossible to deny.

The world of Ragnarök draws from Norse mythology, reimagined through a lens that asks: what if the old stories were not endings, but beginnings? What if the cycles of destruction and renewal that the ancients spoke of were not metaphors, but patterns that echo through every scale of existence?

We offer this work freely because stories want to be told. They want to find readers who will carry them forward, who will argue with the characters, who will imagine what happens in the spaces between scenes. A story locked behind walls is a story half-alive.

If these pages resonate with you, if you find yourself returning to Heim and its inhabitants, know that you are part of the collaboration now. Every reader who engages with a story changes it, completes it, makes it real in a way that words alone cannot.

Welcome to the conversation.
