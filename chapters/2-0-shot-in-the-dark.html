A Shot in the Dark

[Kára]: “I can’t stop the pull. I can only choose how to fall.”

Kara held one arm close to her body while the other was slightly outstretched still warm from Ástríðr’s hand.

The gate flared and Kara found the world black around her. And then a stench she had not had the pleasure of partaking in, in a long time.

"That's fucking disgusting" She muttered.

[Narration]: The fall wasn't graceful. It wasn’t cinematic. It was physics — brutal, real, uncompromising. Kara didn’t fall through the air — she folded into it.

Each tumble was a memory slipping loose. Each bone rattle a question with no time for an answer.

Until — silence.

[Narration]: The mountain greeted her like a long-lost lover who never forgave her for leaving. The impact didn’t echo. It resonated.

[Kára]: “Fffuck.”

Her shoulder screamed. Her ribs moaned. But she was alive — barely.

And then — the stench.

[Narration]: A thick, sour fog wrapped itself around her nostrils. Like rot marinated in ammonia and despair.

[Kára]: "That's fucking disgusting." She said again with more disgust in her voice.

She spat. Or tried to. Her mouth was too dry. She rolled, groaning, dragging herself toward a jagged edge of shade that might pass for shelter.

Something skittered in the dark. Not near. But watching.
